# <div class=&quot;heading-anchor anchor waypoint&quot; id=&quot;what-are-organizations&quot;></div><div class=&quot;heading-text&quot;><div id=&quot;section-what-are-organizations&quot; class=&quot;heading-anchor_backwardsCompatibility&quot;></div>What are Organizations</div><Link aria-label=&quot;Skip link to What are Organizations&quot; class=&quot;heading-anchor-icon fa fa-anchor&quot; href=&quot;#what-are-organizations&quot;></Link>

<p>When you create a business account, you create an Organization. This structure allows for multiple people to manage Profiles, Devices, view Analytics and more. </p>
<p>You may want a business account if you need to do the following:</p>

- Manage large amounts of end user devices or networks

- Quickly onboard hundreds or thousands of devices using RMM

- Grant access to members of your team to be able to view or modify configurations 

- Group Profiles and Devices into Sub-Organizations

- Resell value added services to your own customers 

- Lock down your resolvers to specific IP addresses 

- Share Profiles between organizations

- Whole lot more

<p>In the My Organization tab, you can manage basic details of your company and make customizations. These include:</p>

- Update basic details and contact information 

- Change your data storage region

- Delete all Analytics data

- Enable mandatory 2FA/MFA for all admins

- Invite team members to manage your organization&#x27;s assets

- Create Sub-Organizations that can represent customers, departments, etc and manage their assets separately or delegate access
