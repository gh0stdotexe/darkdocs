<p>Yes, if you use Secure DNS protocols like DNS-over-HTTPS or DNS-over-TLS, DNSSEC provides virtually no value. Your DNS requests cannot be intercepted or spoofed by anyone. Also, since Control D is a manipulating DNS resolver (based on your rules) this is conflicting with the whole concept of DNSSEC. </p>

<p>Yes, if you use Secure DNS protocols like DNS-over-HTTPS or DNS-over-TLS, DNSSEC provides virtually no value. Your DNS requests cannot be intercepted or spoofed by anyone. Also, since Control D is a manipulating DNS resolver (based on your rules) this is conflicting with the whole concept of DNSSEC. </p>
